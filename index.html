Press Release

Upcoming in February 2022, the Ridgefield Guild of Artists is pleased to bring back a small group show. Five artists, as selected by a juror, will share the entire downstairs gallery. Two artists will go in the front gallery, one in the middle and two in the third, thus the 2-1-2 title of the exhibition. The show was curated by Georgette Gouveia. Editor in Chief, WAG Magazine, an award winning luxury lifestyles publication of the Westchester and Fairfield County.

One of the artists in the exhibition is Newtown artist Roberta Shea. Atmosphere and luminance color are the key aspects of Shea’s paintings. With bold, broad brushstrokes she creates a sense of place with paintings full of light, air, atmosphere, and mystery. Through observation she unveils many ways to see the landscape. “What is most important to me, says Shea, is letting the paint be the protagonist to direct the narrative. I paint because I love paint, the feel of it when the brush meets the canvas and the colors. Playing with color still gives me a thrill when one color is placed next to another and it sings.” Seasonal change occurring in nature becomes my inspiration. Because my landscapes are often an exploration of place, I cannot ignore the weather-causing changes happening around me.

The public is invited to an opening reception on Saturday, February 12, 2022, from 4 - 6 pm, 34 Halpin Lane, Ridgefield, CT from 4 - 6pm. The exhibit will run at the Guild from February 12 and close February 27, 2022.
